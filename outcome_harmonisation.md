# Outcome harmonisation notes

The primary outcome was sustained perinatal asphyxia, defined using a Delphi-consensus composite of clinical, biochemical, and neurologic criteria. NICU admission alone, when unrelated to suspected intrapartum compromise or ongoing neonatal resuscitation, should not be treated as a qualifying event.

A case was classified positive if one or more qualifying criteria were present, including:

- stillbirth attributable to perinatal asphyxia;
- neonatal death attributable to perinatal asphyxia;
- hypoxic-ischaemic encephalopathy based on compatible imaging, multisystem organ failure, or therapeutic cooling;
- neonatal resuscitation at 10 minutes followed by NICU admission;
- Apgar score <=6 at 10 minutes or <=4 at 5 minutes;
- arterial cord pH <7.05 or base excess <-12 mmol/L;
- neonatal seizures immediately following birth thought to be due to asphyxia.

Cohort-specific availability of these components should be documented before analysis. Where only objective biochemical/Apgar markers are available, use the harmonised cohort-specific outcome rule and interpret validation estimates accordingly.
